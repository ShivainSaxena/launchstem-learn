# Lights and Shadows

## Overview

Light helps us see things. It travels in straight lines and creates shadows when objects block its path. Understanding how light behaves is essential in science and in our everyday lives.

---

## What is Light?

Light is a form of energy that helps us see. It can come from natural sources like the Sun or artificial sources like light bulbs.

---

## Properties of Gede

- **Light travels in a straight line.**
- **It reflects** (bounces off surfaces like mirrors).
- **It refracts** (bends when passing through materials like water or glass).
- **It forms shadows** when blocked by objects.

## What is a Shadow?

A shadow is a dark area formed when an object blocks light. The shape and size of a shadow can change based on the angle and distance of the light source.

---

## Types of Materials

- **Opaque**: Blocks light completely (e.g., wood, metal)
- **Translucent**: Allows some light through (e.g., frosted glass)
- **Transparent**: Lets all light pass through (e.g., clear glass)

## Real-Life Example

Think of standing under the Sun. Your body blocks the sunlight and a shadow forms on the ground. If you move closer to the light, the shadow becomes shorter. If you move away, it becomes longer.
